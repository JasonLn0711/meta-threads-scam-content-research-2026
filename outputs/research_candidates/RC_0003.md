# RC_0003: Result Display As A Context-Cost Signal

## Title

Result display without guarantee language increases ambiguity and reviewer burden

## Observation

Batch 0005 contrasted `保證收益 + funnel/contact structure` against broad
`成果展示` without explicit `保證收益`.

The A arm produced higher research yield with lower burden. The B arm produced
no `scam` research labels, but it also was not simply negative: most B-arm cases
were `uncertain` and required thread context or second review.

## Hypothesis

`成果展示` is not a reliable high-value source arm unless paired with an
executable transition, explicit certainty, or another review-stable funnel
anchor.

Without that structure, `成果展示` is better treated as a context-cost signal:
it tells the system that human review may need more thread context, not that the
candidate is safe or high-risk.

## Next Experiment

Create a small contrast-aware scoring loop:

```text
strong source arm: 保證收益 + executable transition
context-cost arm: 成果展示 + needs_thread / no explicit guarantee
```

The initial contrast-aware scoring run has now been generated at
`metrics/contrast_scores/latest.yaml`. Next, use this routing in a fresh small
batch and measure whether context-cost routing preserves high yield per reviewer
hour while reducing unnecessary priority inflation.
