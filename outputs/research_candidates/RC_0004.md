# RC_0004: Contrast-Aware Routing As Reviewer-Effort Control

## Observation

Batch 0006 completed a 12-entry metadata-only routing validation. The
`strong_source_priority` lane produced high yield with low review burden, while
the `result_display_context_review` lane produced uncertainty and required
slower context review.

## Hypothesis

Reviewer routing should be based on the combination of behavior signal and
review burden:

```text
保證收益 + executable transition + low context burden
```

is a high-priority discovery lane, while:

```text
成果展示 + context burden
```

is a slow review lane useful for boundary learning, not a fast source arm.

## Next Experiment

Test whether `result_display_context_review` can be split into a smaller
sub-lane that predicts review burden:

```text
candidate feature hypothesis: context_required_result_display
activation rule: 成果展示 AND needs_thread AND NOT 保證收益
expected effect: reduce broad sparse-cluster over-generalization and improve
slow-lane review planning
```

## Guardrails

- metadata-only candidate records
- no raw Threads content
- no PII
- no URLs, handles, screenshots, browser artifacts, or controlled-store locators
- no embedding-based decisions
- no sparse-schema update without human feature review
